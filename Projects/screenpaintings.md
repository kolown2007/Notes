These are new series debuted at ALT called _Screen Paintings._ These paintings are not directly copied from reality but are inspired by the processes of computer screens—their role as generative pipelines where scraped data, screenshots, and low-level graphics experiments first take shape before being re-materialized. 

They are the result of a combination of web scraping, screenshotting, digital image manipulation, and our new obsession with experimental forays into low-level graphics programming. Portions of these digital creations were then copied and rendered using acrylics, stencils, and transfer techniques. In this way, the screen is not the subject but the engine, a stage where images are born before being translated into paint. 

The resulting paintings were also digitized and integrated into our web-based projects and games, underscoring the recursive loop between digital process and physical artifact.
